## Cross-Meeting Decisions

- Poster task should create reasonable competing viewpoints, not one obvious correction that all participants will rank the same way.
- The initial poster should feel plausible and human-made, with enough reasonable flaws to motivate disagreement.
- Reviewer feedback should be human-authored first; AI may polish wording but should not generate the core opinions from scratch.
- Canva materials should avoid preselecting fonts, hierarchy, or layout decisions that bias participant choices.
- Poster requirements should be explicit and checkable: required event details, at least one image, non-blank background, and relevant NYCU colors.
- Design resources should be externalized into Canva/Slides/library pages with IDs; OmniObserve should mainly record text-based component + action items.
- The research target is not whether participants can understand each other, but whether private ideas, minority viewpoints, and public disagreement emerge and move through the system.

## Implications For #63

Participant familiarity is an analysis covariate because the study depends on social risk. If participants already know each other well, they may publicly disagree without needing private notes, cue support, or idea blocks. For formal sessions that specifically test private expression, recruitment should prefer less-familiar participants; if over-familiar groups are unavoidable, the session summary and analysis must qualify private-channel usage.
